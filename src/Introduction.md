This is a book about Kubernetes concepts. Unlike traditional books, which explain things from the ground up, this book explains based on problem statements.

The book is aimed towards people who already know Kubernetes through traditional books. If you haven't read any of those and do not know Kubernetes, then this book can be confusing to you.
However, please do not worry — take as much as you can, and if you want, enjoy the comics at the end of each chapter.

I wrote this based on the problems I have come across and things that interest me. This will be a living book, and I will refine it with examples. I wrote it for myself. I wanted to understand some things, and what better way than to pursue it by asking questions and finding answers. This will certainly be useful to other engineers and architects who have a pragmatic outlook for engineering problems — who want to understand things and dwell deep.

All these chapters make for very interesting discussion points.

Thank you for reading.
